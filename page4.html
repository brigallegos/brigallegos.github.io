the nightmare that is late stage capitalism





the bay is grossly expensive

and its impossible to live alone working for minimum wage

so when i took this job managing a coffee shop

i thought i was doing what was right for myself financially



what i didn't know was how convoluted the franchise was

and how this job would drain the will to live out of me

70 hour weeks, unpaid overtime, and being paid nearly $$15k less than

what other companies were starting their managers at took a huge toll on me

and after a year in the company, this was the first time i felt i hit rock bottom

my depression and anxiety got unmanageable

i was abusing drugs and alcohol to cope with how low i felt

because i had no clue how to help myself and no resources to do so





shit gets hard when you feel you have no where to turn, and no one to turn to

i left that job only because things at home were deteriorating in my family





this is where the grief begin

the first major loss of my adult life
